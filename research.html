Research

My research develops modern statistical methodology for complex, structured, and high-dimensional data, with emphasis on both theory and computation.

Research interests

  • High-dimensional inference and variable selection
  • Quantile regression and robust estimation
  • Nonparametric and semiparametric regression
  • Functional data analysis
  • Statistical learning and deep learning
  • Statistical computing for modern data problems

Current directions

  • Automatic variable selection in longitudinal quantile regression
  • Deep learning methods for scalar-on-function regression
  • Robust variable selection in high-dimensional nonparametric additive models
  • Inverse regression for spatially distributed functional data
  • Semiparametric modeling of heterogeneous disease progression

Overview

My work sits at the intersection of statistical methodology, computation, and applications. A recurring theme in my research is the development of robust and flexible methods for data that are high-dimensional, dependent, or function-valued. I am particularly interested in problems where classical models are not adequate and new inferential or computational tools are needed.

I also work on methodology motivated by real applications, including biomedical and educational data, where interpretability, robustness, and prediction all matter.
